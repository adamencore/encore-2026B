Accessibility

Inclusivity & accessibility resources

Accommodations and policies that make our performances welcoming for everyone.

Encore Performing Arts is committed to making our performances accessible and inclusive for everyone. We offer a variety of resources and accommodations to ensure a welcoming environment for all attendees. If you have any questions, call (435) 414-0049 or email hello@encorepa.org.

✓Policies

Service animals
We adhere to ADA and state laws regarding service animals. Service animals are always welcome at our events. Emotional support animals and pets are not permitted.
Therapists & caregivers
A therapist or caregiver accompanying a paying attendee who requires assistance receives complimentary admission. Please contact us beforehand to reserve the ticket. This does not extend to nannies or babysitters.
Food & drink
Outside food and drinks are not allowed, except water. Guests are encouraged to enjoy food and drinks before the event. If an exception is needed, please let a staff member know.
Strollers
Strollers are permitted, but we encourage minimizing their use inside the venue due to space limitations.

Resources

Sensory-friendly performances
We host sensory-modified performances for families with sensory processing differences. Admission is free with an RSVP. Learn more about Sensory-Friendly Performances →
Discounts
Senior, student, military, and veteran discounts are available on most productions. Ask our box office at (435) 414-0049 ext. 103.

◈Transportation

Accessible parking
Accessible parking spaces are located near the Electric Theater. Refer to venue-specific parking information for details.
Public transit
The Electric Theater is reachable via local public transit. Check with the local transit authority for schedules and routes.

Facility

Accessible entrance
The Electric Theater has accessible entrances for guests needing elevator access. Please notify us on arrival for assistance.
Elevator
An elevator reaches the classrooms and gallery. Note: there is no elevator access to the theater itself — only the classrooms and gallery.
Restrooms
Wheelchair-accessible restrooms are on the main floor, with baby changing stations in both restrooms. A gender-neutral bathroom is at the far end of the gallery, next to the stairs.
Nursing
A private space for nursing is available upon request.
Water fountain
Accessible drinking fountains are available. If assistance is needed, please ask our staff.
Our Home

The historic Electric Theater

Encore Performing Arts performs regularly at the Electric Theater, an iconic venue that greatly enhances our productions. We're proud to be a frequent performer there — but we don't own the Electric Theater, and we don't handle booking or ticket sales for events other than our own.

Tenant status

Encore is a tenant at the Electric Theater. We rent the space for our performances and events, but we don't own or manage the theater itself.

Event inquiries

For other events at the Electric Theater — including booking and ticket sales — please contact the theater directly. It's owned and operated by St. George City.

Contact

Reach the Electric Theater (City of St. George) at (435) 627-4515.
